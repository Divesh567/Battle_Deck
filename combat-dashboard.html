Core Combat System

How the game is structured, what drives each fight, and the flow of a round.

The Core Loop
Phase 1
Loadout
Pick Character → Class → Weapon → Enemy. Your deck and stats are assembled.
↓
Phase 2
Initiative
Higher initiative goes first. Ties → player wins.
↓
Phase 3
Turn Loop
Alternate turns until one combatant reaches 0 HP.
How Your Deck Is Built
  • Character starting deck — base cards tied to the character asset
  • Class signature cards — cards added by the chosen skill set
  • Weapon-granted cards — cards from the equipped weapon
  • Shuffled together — the combined pile is shuffled at combat start
Every loadout combination produces a different deck. No two fights play the same.
Turn Structure — Each Side

① Start of Turn

  • Armor → reset to max armor
  • Energy → reset to starting energy
  • Draw cards to fill hand
  • Status effects tick (Bleeding deals damage)
  • If Stunned → skip to end of turn

② During Turn

  • Player: Play any cards, any order, while energy allows
  • Cards are moved to discard after play
  • Press End Turn to pass manually
  • Enemy: Executes its next intent automatically
  • Enemy's upcoming move always shown in UI

③ End of Turn

  • Entire hand discarded
  • All status durations tick down by 1
  • Statuses at 0 are removed
  • Turn passes to opponent
  • Turn counter increments
Deck Cycling
  • Draw pile empty? Shuffle the discard pile into a new draw pile
  • Cards never leave the deck during a fight (Exhaust changes this — not yet live)
  • No limit on how many times the deck can cycle per fight
Energy
  • Each card has a fixed energy cost to play
  • Energy resets fully at the start of every turn
  • Unspent energy is lost at turn end (no carry-over)
  • Cards are greyed out when unaffordable
  • Cards can grant or drain energy as an effect
Initiative

Compared once at combat start. Higher value acts first. On a tie the player wins.

Formula: Character base + Class bonus initiative.

Armor

Absorbs incoming damage before HP is touched. Armor is consumed as it absorbs. Pierce Armor effects skip this entirely.

Resets to max armor at the start of every turn.

Enemy Intent System

Enemies use a scripted move list called Intents. By default they cycle in order. Shuffle mode picks randomly. The next move is always telegraphed to the player — a core design pillar.

Damage Resolution Pipeline

Every time a damage effect fires, the engine applies these steps in exact order:

1
Base Value
Start with the flat base value from the effect asset.
damage = baseValue
2
Stat Scaling Not Active
Add scaling contribution from Base Attack, Magic Power, Cards in Hand, etc. System is designed — currently disabled.
+ (stat × multiplier)
3
Weak Modifier
If the attacker has Weak status, multiply damage down.
× 0.75 if Weak
4
Vulnerable Modifier
If the target has Vulnerable status, multiply damage up.
× 1.50 if Vulnerable
5
Elemental Resistance Not Active
Apply target's resistance multiplier for the damage type. Data is modelled; step is disabled.
× resistance
6
Critical Hit
If the effect has Can Crit enabled and a random roll beats the attacker's crit chance.
× 2.0 on crit
7
Round & Clamp
Round to nearest integer. No hit can deal less than 1 damage.
max(1, round(x))
8
Armor Absorption
If effect does not pierce armor, armor absorbs damage first. Armor is reduced by the amount absorbed.
armor soaks first
9
Apply to HP
Remaining damage is subtracted from the target's current HP.
HP -= finalDmg
Steps marked Not Active are fully modelled in data but their calculation is commented out in the engine.

Conditional Effects

A Conditional effect checks a state and branches to a "then" or "else" effect array. Both branches are full effect lists — they can contain further conditionals.
